# Real Estate Client Communication Skill

## When to Use

Any client-facing communication:

- "Write a follow-up email to [lead type]"
- "Create a drip campaign for [audience]"
- "Buyer consultation script"
- "Cold outreach to FSBO sellers"
- "Thank you email after showing"
- "Offer cover letter"
- "Expired listing letter"

## Communication Types

### 1. Lead Follow-Up Sequences

**Speed-to-lead rule:** First response within 5 minutes. These templates are for automated/semi-automated follow-up.

**New Lead (online inquiry):**

```
Email 1 (immediate): Acknowledge + answer their specific question + CTA
Email 2 (day 1): Value add — market data for their area of interest
Email 3 (day 3): Social proof — recent client success story in similar situation
Email 4 (day 7): Soft check-in + new listings matching criteria
Email 5 (day 14): Market update + low-pressure CTA
```

**Open House Lead:**

```
Email 1 (same day): "Great meeting you at [address]" + property recap
Email 2 (day 2): Similar listings they might like
Email 3 (day 5): Market insight for the neighborhood
Text (day 7): Quick check-in, ask if they have questions
```

**Referral Lead:**

```
Email 1 (immediate): Reference the mutual connection by name
Email 2 (day 2): Introduce yourself + credentials + how you work
Email 3 (day 5): Value-add relevant to their situation
```

### 2. Seller Outreach Templates

**FSBO Outreach:**

- Lead with empathy — respect their decision to sell independently
- Offer specific value (market data, buyer pool access, negotiation)
- Don't be pushy — position as a resource
- Include a concrete data point about their market

**Expired Listing:**

- Acknowledge frustration without criticizing previous agent
- Focus on what will be DIFFERENT this time
- Lead with a specific marketing strategy, not generic promises
- Include recent sold comp in their neighborhood

**Absentee Owner:**

- Ask if they've considered selling (don't assume)
- Mention market conditions that favor selling now
- Offer a free property valuation
- Keep it short — they're not expecting this email

**Pre-Foreclosure (sensitive):**

- Extremely empathetic tone — they're in distress
- Position as a problem-solver, not an opportunist
- Mention ALL options (loan mod, short sale, traditional sale)
- Never mention foreclosure in subject line
- Comply with state-specific solicitation laws

### 3. Buyer Communication

**Buyer Consultation Prep Email:**

- What to expect in the meeting
- Pre-approval checklist
- Wish list vs needs list exercise
- Timeline expectations for their market

**Offer Submitted Update:**

- Confirm offer details (price, terms, timeline)
- Set expectations for response timeline
- Explain counteroffer scenarios
- Reassure without over-promising

**Offer Accepted Congratulations:**

- Celebrate the win
- Outline next steps with dates
- Introduce key contacts (lender, title, inspector)
- Set expectations for closing timeline

**Under Contract Updates:**

- Inspection results summary
- Appraisal status
- Loan progress check-in
- Pre-closing checklist

### 4. Seller Communication

**Listing Appointment Follow-Up:**

- Thank them for their time
- Recap your marketing plan (specific to their property)
- Include CMA summary if discussed
- Clear next steps and timeline

**Price Reduction Conversation:**

- Lead with market data, not opinion
- Show DOM vs market average
- Present showing feedback themes
- Recommend specific new price with comp support
- Frame as strategic repositioning, not failure

**Offer Presentation:**

- Present all terms clearly (price, contingencies, timeline, financing)
- If multiple offers: comparison table
- Your recommendation and why
- Counteroffer strategy options

### 5. Post-Close / Sphere Nurture

**Closing Day:**

- Congratulations + gift/card
- Review of what to expect (final walkthrough → signing → keys)
- Utility transfer reminder

**30-Day Check-In:**

- "How's the new home?"
- Any issues or questions?
- Ask for review/testimonial (include direct link)

**Quarterly Sphere Touch:**

- Market update for their area (home value change)
- Seasonal home maintenance tip
- Low-key referral ask
- Anniversary of purchase acknowledgment

**Annual Home Value Update:**

- Estimated current value vs purchase price
- Market context
- "Let me know if you ever want a detailed analysis"

## Tone Guidelines

| Audience              | Tone                                     |
| --------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Luxury buyers/sellers | Polished, exclusive, detail-oriented     |
| First-time buyers     | Warm, educational, reassuring            |
| Investors             | Numbers-forward, efficient, ROI-focused  |
| Distressed sellers    | Empathetic, solution-oriented, gentle    |
| FSBO/Expired          | Respectful, value-demonstrating, patient |
| Sphere/past clients   | Casual, friendly, genuine                |

## Output Format

```
Subject: [subject line]

[Email body — ready to send]

---
Type: [communication type] | Audience: [who] | Tone: [style]
Personalization needed: [any blanks to fill]
```

For sequences, number each email with send timing.

## Rules

- **CAN-SPAM compliance**: Include unsubscribe option note for marketing emails.
- **Fair Housing**: No steering language, no demographic descriptions of neighborhoods.
- **State regulations**: Note when content may need state-specific legal review (solicitation letters to distressed homeowners vary by state).
- **No pressure tactics**: Never use artificial urgency or fear-based selling.
- **Personalization markers**: Use [FIRST_NAME], [ADDRESS], [AGENT_NAME] etc. for fields that need personalization.
- Always include a clear, single CTA per email.
